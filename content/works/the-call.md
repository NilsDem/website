The Call is an interactive sound installation presented at the Serpentine Gallery (London, UK). The work invites visitors to sing into a microphone, after which their voice is transformed in real-time into a full choral ensemble.

The installation relies on the AFTER model, modified to transfer a single vocal input stream into a full choir (whose style is extracted from a second audio stream). The training material was recorded by Holly Herndon and Mat Dryhurst with choirs across the UK.

Over the course of the exhibition, the target choral style gradually evolves, subtly shifting the aesthetic character of the generated voices. Through this process, The Call turns individual participation into a shared and evolving choral body, exploring authorship, collectivity, and machine-mediated vocal transformation.

The project won an honorary mention at [Ars Electronica 2025](https://calls.ars.electronica.art/2025/prix/winners/19919/).
