Imago is a standalone generative audio instrument built around the AFTER model, developed in collaboration with Kieran Fiechan and Domenico di Paolo. Conceived as both a musical instrument and an interactive object, Imago embeds custom generative models trained on the sonic worlds of artists including Robert Laidlow, Molécule, and Canblaster.

The device features an interactive tactile surface that allows performers to navigate the timbral space of each model in real time. Powered by an embedded NVIDIA Jetson Nano, Imago operates as a fully standalone hardware system capable of running multiple model instances simultaneously with low latency.

Still under development, the project was presented during Milan Design Week 2026.
