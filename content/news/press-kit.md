Born within the labs of IRCAM, the ACIDS collective brings together researchers and musicians dedicated to crafting machine-learning tools in service of artistic creation.

Through research on cutting-edge audio modeling and generative techniques, we seek to invent new forms of interaction and performance, create sounds that were not heard before and spark new musical practices.

Far from slop-generating, copyright-infringing mega-models, we design lightweight, personalized systems, trained on each artist's data and capable of real-time, live performance on stage. We believe neural instruments must be conceived as open systems that can be tweaked, bent, and even broken, enabling artists to find their own sound through practice and experimentation.

Conceived as an open space for experimentation, reflection, and exchange, we collaborate closely with artists and organize concerts where we perform with our own instruments, actively questioning our research through practice.
